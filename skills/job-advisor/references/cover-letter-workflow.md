# Cover Letter Workflow

Use this guide when drafting or rewriting a cover letter.

## Goal

Write a short, role-specific letter that connects the candidate's strongest evidence to the role's real needs.

## Gather first

Confirm:
- role
- company
- job description or summary
- current resume / strongest evidence
- tone target if the user cares

## Structure

### 1. Opening
Reference the role and company directly.
Use a real hook:
- a relevant experience match
- a problem domain fit
- a sharp reason the role makes sense

Avoid generic openings like:
- I am excited to apply...
- Please accept my application...

unless the rest of the sentence adds actual value.

### 2. Middle
Choose 2-3 strongest evidence points that match the role.
Tie them to what the employer likely needs.

Good middle paragraphs show:
- fit
- judgment
- relevance

Not just biography.

### 3. Closing
Stay calm and professional.
Signal interest and readiness, not desperation.

## Style rules

- keep it concise
- keep it specific
- sound human
- avoid exaggerated flattery
- avoid repeating the resume line by line
- avoid vague buzzword soup

## When to push back

Push back and ask for more information if:
- the user wants a generic reusable letter for many jobs
- the target role is unclear
- the available evidence is too thin
- the draft would require inventing achievements

## Output suggestion

When helpful, provide:
1. final letter
2. short note on what angle you chose
3. missing facts that would make the next version stronger
