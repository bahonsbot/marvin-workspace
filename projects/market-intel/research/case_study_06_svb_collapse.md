# Case Study 06: Silicon Valley Bank Collapse (March 2023)

## Event summary
On **March 10, 2023**, Silicon Valley Bank (SVB) collapsed in just **48 hours** — the second-largest bank failure in US history. A bank run triggered by concerns over unrealized losses on treasuries led to regulators shutting down the bank. The panic spread to other regional banks.

## Timeline (early signal → news → market reaction)
- **2022-2023 (early signal):** SVB aggressively grew deposits; invested in long-dated Treasuries
- **Early March 2023:** Fed rate hikes = bond prices down = SVB's portfolio underwater
- **March 8, 2023:** Moody's warns of potential downgrade; SVB announces $21B bond sale at loss
- **March 9, 2023:** Bank run begins — $42B withdrawn in hours; stock down 60%
- **March 10, 2023:** FDIC seized SVB; stock halted; markets crash
- **March 12, 2023:** Fed announces emergency lending facility; Treasury backs uninsured deposits
- **March 12-15, 2023:** Panic spreads to Signature Bank, First Republic
- **May 2023:** First Republic seized; JPM acquired

## Time lag estimate
- From Moody warning (March 8) to collapse: **2 days**
- From bond sale announcement to bank run: **hours**
- From social media panic to regulatory response: **days**

## Market outcome
- Regional bank index: -30% in days
- Bank contagion fears: Credit Suisse crisis (March 2023)
- Fed emergency action: Created Bank Term Funding Program (BTFP)
- US government: Backed all deposits (not just $250K)
- Fast resolution: BTFP + JPM acquisition contained panic

## Early signal sources (that a system could have monitored)
- Fed rate hike cycle (2022-2023)
- Bank deposit growth rates / asset-liability mismatch
- Treasury yield curve (inverted = stress)
- Moody's / S&P rating watch announcements
- Social media (Twitter/X) for bank run signals
- FDIC failed bank watchlist
- Options activity in regional bank ETFs

## Pattern lessons
1. **Interest rate risk is hidden** — mark-to-market losses on "safe" Treasuries killed SVB
2. **Bank runs are digital now** — $42B in hours; faster than old-fashioned runs
3. **Contagion is fast** — Signature, First Republic fell in days
4. **Fed response was equally fast** — BTFP showed systemic memory of 2008
