# Case Study 07: Evergrande/China Real Estate Crisis (2021-)

## Event summary
In **2021**, China Evergrande — the world's most indebted property developer ($300B+ debt) — began collapsing under a mountain of debt. The crisis revealed systemic risks in China's property sector and sparked concerns about contagion to global markets.

## Timeline (early signal → news → market reaction)
- **2020 (early signal):** China introduces "three red lines" to curb property debt
- **Aug 2021:** Evergrande misses bond payments; ratings downgraded
- **Sept 2021:** Evergrande misses another payment; investors protest
- **Oct 15, 2021:** China government comments; says "contagion controllable"
- **Oct 19, 2021:** Sinic Construction defaults ($246M) — first Chinese default
- **Dec 2021:** Evergrande formally defaults
- **2022-2023:** Property crisis deepens; Country Garden also struggles
- **2023:** Evergrande liquidation ordered; market impact continues

## Time lag estimate
- From "three red lines" policy (Aug 2020) to crisis: **~1 year**
- From first missed payment (Sept 2021) to default: **months**
- From China statements to global market reaction: **days to weeks**

## Market outcome
- Evergrande stock: -90%+ from peak
- China property bonds: collapsed
- Hong Kong/China stocks: impacted but limited global spillover
- Global markets: contained; China policy support limited wider contagion
- Lesson: China contained it, but property sector remains drag on Chinese economy

## Early signal sources (that a system could have monitored)
- China policy announcements (PBOC, government)
- Property developer bond yields (spreads widening)
- Land sale data (local government finance)
- Credit default swaps (CDS spreads)
- China developer default news / ratings
- Social media (Chinese platforms, investor forums)

## Pattern lessons
1. **Policy-driven crisis** — China's "three red lines" triggered the unwind
2. **Slow-motion default** — took months/years to fully resolve
3. **Contained but significant** — limited global spillover, major domestic impact
4. **China gov't controls outcome** — unlike Western crises, state can dictate resolution
