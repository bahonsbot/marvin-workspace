# Case Study 10: Tesla Stock Split & Earnings Surprises (2020)

## Event summary
In **2020**, Tesla executed a 5-for-1 stock split and delivered consistent earnings surprises that drove the stock from ~$100 (pre-split ~$500) to ~$900 by year end. Tesla's inclusion in the S&P 500 later that year amplified its market impact.

## Timeline (early signal → news → market reaction)
- **2020 (early signal):** Tesla consistently beats earnings; delivery numbers strong
- **July 2020:** Tesla announces Q2 profit (first profitable quarter)
- **Aug 11, 2020:** Tesla announces 5-for-1 stock split
- **Aug 31, 2020:** Stock split takes effect; stock surges 12%
- **Oct 2020:** Tesla wins first full year profit; S&P 500 inclusion announced
- **Dec 2020:** Stock split effect + S&P 500 inclusion = massive rally
- **2020 performance:** +740% for the year

## Time lag estimate
- From earnings surprise to stock move: **days to weeks**
- From split announcement to effective date: **~3 weeks**
- From S&P inclusion announcement to actual inclusion: **~6 weeks**

## Market outcome
- TSLA: $100 → $900 in 2020 (+740%)
- Added to S&P 500 (Dec 2020) — forced index funds to buy
- EV sector: Rivian, Lucid IPOs followed
- EV stocks broadly rallied on Tesla mania

## Early signal sources (that a system could have monitored)
- Earnings whisper numbers vs actual
- Delivery guidance vs actual
- Production capacity announcements
- Analyst upgrades/downgrades
- Options activity (IV crush patterns)
- S&P 500 inclusion rumors

## Pattern lessons
1. **Earnings beats = momentum** — Tesla's consistent beats built multi-year rally
2. **Stock splits drive retail interest** — splitting makes shares "affordable"
3. **Index inclusion = forced buying** — S&P 500 inclusion created massive demand
4. **Sector rotation** — Tesla mania spilled over to EV peers
