# Case Study 04: COVID-19 Market Crash (March 2020)

## Event summary
On **March 2020**, the COVID-19 pandemic triggered the fastest market crash in history. The S&P 500 fell **34% in 33 days** from its February peak. Multiple circuit breakers were triggered — an extremely rare event.

## Timeline (early signal → news → market reaction)
- **Jan 2020 (early signal):** First COVID-19 cases reported in China; WHO situation reports begin
- **Late Feb 2020:** Virus spreads beyond China; Italy reports outbreaks; markets start declining
- **9 Mar 2020:** First of **four circuit breaker triggers** (S&P 500 dropped 7% — level 1 circuit breaker activated)
- **12 Mar 2020:** Second circuit breaker — worst single-day drop since 1987 Black Monday
- **16 Mar 2020:** Third circuit breaker — **worst single-day decline since 1940** (12.9%)
- **18 Mar 2020:** Fourth circuit breaker
- **23 Mar 2020:** S&P 500 hits bottom (2,191) — down 34% from Feb peak
- **Oil price war:** Saudi-Russia price war erupted March 7, pushing WTI to **negative $38/bbl** on Apr 20

## Time lag estimate
- From WHO emergency declaration (Jan 30) to initial market decline: **~3 weeks**
- From Italy outbreak (late Feb) to circuit breakers: **days to 1 week**
- Peak panic to bottom: **~1 week**

## Market outcome
- S&P 500: -34% in 33 days (fastest crash ever)
- WTI crude: crashed to -$38/bbl (first negative in history)
- Volatility index (VIX): spiked to 82.69 (highest ever)
- Quick recovery: S&P 500 recovered to pre-crash levels by August 2020

## Early signal sources (that a system could have monitored)
- WHO situation reports (daily)
- China travel data / flight tracking
- Italy outbreak news
- Oil production negotiations / Saudi-Russia relations
- VIX futures / options activity
- Treasury yield spreads

## Pattern lessons
1. **Pandemic + supply shock = compounding crash** — dual shock (health + oil) worse than either alone
2. **Speed matters** — fastest crash ever; 4 circuit breakers in 9 days (unprecedented)
3. **Recovery was equally fast** — gov't stimulus + Fed action reversed crash in weeks
4. **Oil was the accelerant** — Saudi-Russia price war added another shock layer
